Anyone can buy in EU
No EU residency or ILR needed to purchase. US citizens, UK residents, anyone — hire a local notaire, get a local tax number, transfer funds. Your friend's mom bought in Salies exactly this way.
What ILR changes
Spending time there, not buying rights. Pre-ILR: trips abroad >180 days/yr break your qualifying clock — own the property but keep stays short. Post-ILR: no qualifying rules, spend months at a time freely. Only limit: don't leave UK for >2 consecutive years or you lose ILR.
UK SDLT trap — sequence matters
Own anything abroad → lose first-time buyer SDLT relief + 3% surcharge on UK purchase. £12–15k extra on a £400k flat. Buy UK first, then EU. No getting around this.

ABBV Share Incentive Plan · How it works

Why this is good · £150/mo comes out of your salary before income tax and NI — so at your rate, £63 of every £150 is tax you never hand over. You effectively pay £87 in take-home for £150 of shares. AbbVie then matches 1:1, adding another free £150/mo. Total: £300/mo · £3,600/yr accumulating with zero action needed from you.
While you wait · Shares sit in a trustee account on a platform (check your AbbVie benefit portal). Nothing to do — just let them accumulate. Each batch has two milestones: a 3-year vesting mark (shares unconditionally yours even if you leave AbbVie) and a 5-year tax-free mark (withdraw with zero income tax, zero NI). Nov 2024 batch → vested Nov 2027, tax-free Nov 2029. The 5-year mark is the target.
When to sell · Don't sell each batch as it clears — you'd be logging in monthly forever. Do an annual sweep instead: once a year in Nov/Dec, log in and sell everything that's cleared its 5-year mark since your last sweep. 10 minutes, one transaction per year. Move proceeds to Fidelity via Wise as usual. Sell most or all each time — you're already AbbVie-exposed through your salary and pension, so no need to hold extra here.

1 batch will have cleared its 5-year mark by then (Nov 2024 · £300 at acquisition). From Nov/Dec 2030 it's steady state: 12 batches · £3,600 per sweep, every Nov/Dec.

UK tax: After 5 years, no income tax and no NI on the shares — that's the whole point of the plan. The only possible charge is CGT on any price gain since purchase, and at this scale (£3,600/yr in shares) it's very unlikely to exceed your £3k annual exemption. Effectively £0 UK tax.

US tax — what you need to know: The US doesn't recognise UK SIP rules, so every time you sell shares you owe US tax on the profit. Profit = sale price minus what the shares were worth when you got them (your "cost basis"). Since you'll hold 3+ years before selling, it's taxed at the lower long-term capital gains rate (15%). You report it on Schedule D in your normal annual US return — nothing extra beyond what you already file.

One thing to do now: Log into your SIP platform (AbbVie benefit portal) and confirm it shows the purchase price for each monthly batch going back to Nov 2024. That's the number you'll need in 2029 to calculate your US profit. If it doesn't store it clearly, start keeping your own note. * CGT could apply if total gains across all assets exceed £3k in a tax year.

Bottom line — you're still winning by a lot: Your real monthly outlay is £87 (£150 reduced by 42% — 40% higher-rate IT + 2% NI saved via salary sacrifice) = £1,044/year actual cash.
