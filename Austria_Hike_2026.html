Austria Ridge Trek
Hut-to-Hut · July 2026

A WWI ridge trail that's genuinely off the tourist circuit. Three nights of Alpenverein huts along the Austria–Italy border, with the Sextener Dolomites on one side and the Carnic valleys on the other. Four-hour hiking days, optional summits, and a landscape that most people driving through the Alps have never seen.

1 Mon

Sillian → Sexten → Sillianer Hütte

Monday 27 July 📏 ~7.8 km ↑ ~480m from chairlift ⏱ ~2–2.5h from chairlift top Moderate Ridge Start
Sillian 🚂 Sexten (20 min direct train) → Helmjet chairlift (~1,968m top) → Austrian border ridge → Sillianer Hütte (2,447m)
  • The Helmjet Sexten chairlift is the smart way to start — it saves roughly 700m of vertical gain and 2+ hours. Buy your ticket in Sexten village and ride up to ~1,968m. A matter of preference whether to hike the whole thing, but the real trail starts from the top and the chairlift is genuinely a normal way to begin this section
  • From the chairlift top you hit the main ridge almost immediately. The border between Italy and Austria runs along the crest — you're literally standing on it for much of the day
  • Trail marker: Route 403 (Carnic Peace Trail / Friedensweg). Follow it east from the chairlift and you'll stay on the main ridge all the way to the hut
  • The landscape opens up fast: the Sextener Dolomites (Drei Zinnen / Tre Cime visible on a clear day) sit to the south across the Italian side; the gentler green Carnic valleys drop away to the north into Austria
  • WWI is everywhere on this trail. The border ridge was one of the most strategic and brutal front lines of the entire war — Austrian and Italian soldiers fought at altitudes above 2,500m through multiple winters. Gun emplacements, wire remnants, and carved tunnels are visible from Day 1. The Peace Trail designation is intentional
  • The section to Sillianer Hütte is the shortest hiking day, good for settling into the rhythm and testing your pacing
Optional summit from Day 1: Eisenleiten or nearby ridgeline peaks above Sillianer Hütte offer an additional 45–90 minutes for anyone with energy after arriving at the hut. Leave your pack at the hut first.

2 Tue

Sillianer Hütte → Obstanserseehütte

Tuesday 28 July 📏 ~8.5 km ↑ ~641m cumulative gain ⏱ ~3.5–4h Moderate–Hard Optional Summits
Sillianer Hütte (2,447m) → Eisenreich (2,665m) optional summit → Obstanserseehütte (2,306m)
  • Day 2 is the most varied — the ridge gains more height than Day 1, and the terrain includes sections with fixed cables and short ladders. None of it requires technical gear, but sure-footedness matters. No one should be in sandals or trail runners on this section
  • Eisenreich (2,665m) is the high point of the route and it's achievable as a slight detour above the main trail. Takes an extra 45–60 minutes return but the view from the summit is the payoff — the full Dolomites panorama to the south and the Carnic valley system to the north
  • The trail passes several WWI fortification sites — look for the carved tunnels and remnants of wire positions built into the rock face. Some sections have interpretive markers
  • The Obstansersee (alpine lake) appears below the hut as you descend toward it in the afternoon. The hut sits above the lake with views across to the Dolomites ridge. In July the light on the lake in the late afternoon is notable
  • Day 2 is a medium day — not the longest, but the most technically interesting. Start by 08:00 to arrive by early afternoon with time for the optional summit
Depart Sillianer by 08:00. Afternoon storms can build on the ridge above 2,500m. The fixed cable sections are manageable in dry conditions; wet rock changes the calculation. An early start keeps you ahead of any weather that builds after noon.

3 Wed

Obstanserseehütte → Porzehütte

Wednesday 29 July 📏 ~12 km ↑ ~660m cumulative ⏱ ~5–6h Longest Day Optional Summits
Obstanserseehütte (2,306m) → Hollbrucker Spitze optional → Carnic Ridge → Porzehütte (1,942m)
  • The longest and most committing day. 12km of ridge walking between two huts, with a cumulative elevation profile rather than one big climb — you'll be going up and down multiple times across several ridge points
  • The route continues east along the Carnic Main Ridge (Austria–Italy border). The views stay extraordinary throughout — both sides of the border visible simultaneously from most high points
  • Hollbrucker Spitze and surrounding peaks are available as optional detours; these are proper summits requiring an extra 60–90 minutes each but accessible without technical gear from the main trail
  • The descent to Porzehütte in the final section is the longest downhill of the trip — knees will know they've done something. Poles recommended. The hut appears below you as you work down from the ridge
  • Porzehütte at 1,942m is lower than the previous two huts — the air feels noticeably warmer. It's also the newest of the three (rebuilt 2020). Large enough that you can spread out, good kitchen
Early start essential — depart Obstansersee by 07:30. Day 3 is the longest day and has the most exposure on the ridge. Weather builds from early afternoon in July. At 12km with 660m of gain/loss, arriving after 15:00 in deteriorating weather is not comfortable. Leave by 07:30 and you'll arrive well before any afternoon storms.
Headlamp in your day pack tonight. Porzehütte has access to one of the WWI tunnel networks — if you explore them on Day 4 morning before descending, you'll need it. Worth it.

July Weather on the Carnic Ridge

At Ridge Altitude (2,000–2,600m)

50–64°F
Daytime highs · warmest weeks
  • Nights at huts: 39–50°F — layers essential
  • Afternoon thunderstorms build fast, especially Days 2 and 3
  • Ridge exposed: rain gear always in pack, not in hotel bag
  • Snowpatches possible in shaded gullies until mid-July

General July Pattern

Stable mornings
Weather window: 06:00–13:00
  • July is peak season — warmest and driest month
  • The Austria–Italy border ridge catches weather from both sides
  • Morning departures by 07:30–08:00 get the best conditions
  • Check: ZAMG (Austrian weather) + meteo.it

Packing List

T3 grade — stiff boots and poles are non-negotiable. No trail runners on this route. Day packs only (~30L): everything else stays at the Sillian hotel.

Boots & Poles

  • Stiff hiking boots, ankle support
  • Broken in before the trip
  • Trekking poles (both hands for cable sections)
  • Gaiters (optional, useful if wet)
  • Spare laces

Clothing

  • Merino base layer × 2
  • Fleece or light down jacket
  • Waterproof shell (not just windshell)
  • Hiking trousers × 2
  • Warm hat + gloves
  • Hut slippers / camp shoes
  • Merino socks × 3

Day Pack

  • 2L water + purification tabs
  • Packed lunch (order night before)
  • Headlamp + spare batteries
  • Sun cream + lip balm (ridge = full exposure)
  • Emergency space blanket
  • Rain cover for pack

Overnight

  • Sleep sack / liner (huts require it)
  • Small towel
  • First aid: blister pads, Compeed, pain relief, tape
  • Toothbrush + minimal toiletries
  • Earplugs (dorms)
  • Phone charger + power bank

Docs & Admin

  • Passport (US: confirm ETIAS done)
  • Travel insurance card
  • €200+ cash per person
  • Kompass map Sheet 47 (buy in Sillian)
  • Komoot / Outdooractive downloaded offline
  • Hut ref numbers saved offline
Cash in Austria. Alpenverein huts typically accept cash (euros) and sometimes cards. Bring €200+ per person in cash. ATMs in Sexten and San Candido; none on the trail or in Obertilliach village.
